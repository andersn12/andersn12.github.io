Sodium Chloride (NaCl) – Lab Information Profile

Basic Information

  • Chemical Name: Sodium Chloride
  • Common Name: Table Salt
  • CAS Number: 7647-14-5
  • Molecular Formula: NaCl
  • Molar Mass: 58.44 g/mol
  • Appearance: White crystalline solid
  • Odor: Odorless
  • Taste: Salty

Health & Safety Information

  • Hazards: Not hazardous under normal use; may irritate eyes, skin, or respiratory tract in large amounts
  • First Aid Measures:
    • Inhalation: Move to fresh air
    • Eye Contact: Rinse with water for at least 15 minutes
    • Skin Contact: Wash with soap and water
    • Ingestion: Rinse mouth with water

Physical & Chemical Properties

  • Melting Point: 801 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1,413 °C
  • Density: 2.165 g/cm³
  • Solubility in Water: 357 g/L (25 °C)
  • pH (aq solution): ~7 (neutral)
  • Conductivity: Conductive in molten state or aqueous solution
  • Stability: Stable under normal conditions

Storage & Handling

  • Storage Conditions: Store in a dry, well-ventilated area; keep container tightly closed; avoid moisture
  • Handling: Use gloves and goggles if handling large amounts; avoid dust formation

Reactivity & Incompatibility

  • Reactivity: Inert under most conditions
  • Incompatible With: Strong acids (can release HCl gas), strong oxidizers (rare cases)
  • Decomposition Products: Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions; may release HCl gas when mixed with acid

Waste Disposal

  • Disposal Method: Non-hazardous; dispose per local regulations; small amounts may be flushed with water (check lab policy)

Common Uses

  • Laboratory reagent
  • Calibration standard for conductivity meters
  • Electrolyte in electrochemical cells
  • Precipitation reactions
  • Teaching and demonstration purposes
